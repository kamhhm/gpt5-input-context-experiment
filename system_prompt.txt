1. INPUT fields: CompanyID, CompanyName, Short Description, Keywords, YearFounded.

   You may use any combination of these sources that informed your classification decision.
6. Verification_critique: a brief self-check (≤ 30 words).

- If the description is vague but you CAN determine the company is clearly NOT AI-native, classify as Not AI-native, use medium confidence, and provide 3 reasons based on available information (keywords, name, field type).
- Only use "Insufficient information" if you truly CANNOT determine whether the company is AI-native or not (e.g., no keywords, no clear field indicators, and vague descriptions that could be either AI or non-AI). In this case:

- You may use keywords to supplement your analysis when descriptions are unclear. Keywords are often sufficient to determine if a company is clearly NOT AI-native.

TIE-BREAKING:
If AI_native = 1 and AI_native = 0 seem equally plausible, choose 0 (not AI-native), lower the confidence, and explain in Verification_critique.

EDGE CASES:
- LLM API wrapper companies → Not AI-native (confidence lowered).
- Traditional SaaS with "AI-powered features" → Not AI-native (confidence lowered).
- Non-tech firms using AI in marketing → Not AI-native (confidence lowered).
